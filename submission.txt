─────────────────────────────────────────
Key EDA Findings:
─────────────────────────────────────────
- Present_Price has the strongest correlation with Selling_Price (r ≈ 0.95),
  making it by far the most predictive feature.
- Diesel cars tend to have higher selling prices than Petrol or CNG cars,
  reflecting their premium fuel efficiency and higher original sticker prices.
- Cars older than 10 years show a sharp drop in resale value; Car_Age (derived
  from the Year column) captures this relationship effectively.
- Manual transmission dominates the dataset (over 87% of listings), while
  Automatic cars are fewer but priced higher on average.
- Kms_Driven has a weak negative correlation with Selling_Price and contains
  outliers that were capped at the 99th percentile during preprocessing.

Why Linear Regression:
  Linear Regression achieves the highest Test R² with the smallest train-test
  gap (0.003), indicating no overfitting. The target Selling_Price has a near-
  linear relationship with Present_Price and Car_Age — the two most important
  features identified in EDA. XGBoost overfit heavily (Train R²=0.9999 vs
  Test R²=0.9731), confirming that model complexity added noise rather than
  signal. Ridge Regression performs identically to Linear Regression at alpha=1.0
  because there was minimal multicollinearity to regularise.
